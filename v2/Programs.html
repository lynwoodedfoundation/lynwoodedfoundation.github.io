Programs and News from Lynwood Unified

Hour of Code is coming to Lynwood Unified!


Our world is surrounded by technology. Information, commerce, communication, and entertainment all rely on computers. But only a tiny fraction of us learn computer science, the basics of how computers work, or how to create software, apps, or web sites. This year, to celebrate Computer Science Education Week (Dec 7-13), Lynwood Unified students will be participating in a one-hour coding activity during the school day along with other children from around the world in a massive campaign to prepare our population for the 21st century. The Hour of Code campaign has an audacious goal: to introduce 10 million students to one hour of computer science. Our Hour of Code is a statement that Lynwood Unified is ready to teach these foundational 21st century skills. To learn more about The Hour of Code, visit http://hourofcode.org for details, and help spread the word.

Firebaugh’s IB Programme Gives Students College Credits


With a mission to promote intercultural understanding, critical thinking and independent learning, Firebaugh High School issued its first International Baccalaureate diplomas in 2008, when it was classified as an IB World School. Since then, dozens of students have obtained IB diplomas, earned college credits and gone on to such prestigious colleges and universities as Columbia, MIT, Wellesley, Williams and, as well as joining the UC ranks in Los Angeles, Berkeley and Irvine. The rigorous, two-year college- and career-preparatory curriculum for juniors and seniors allows students to take courses including Biology, Chemistry, English, History, Mandarin, Spanish, Mathematics, and Visual Arts.
To earn a full IB diploma, students must pass IB exams for each course, complete an interdisciplinary course and extended essay. The 2014-15 school year marks the most participation in the program’s history at the school. One hundred sixty-two students took nearly 400 exams in multiple subjects in May 2015 and the school doubled the number of IB diplomas awarded to students from the previous year. The IB Diploma Programme (DP) began in 1968 and is offered to high school students ages 16-19. More than 70,000 educators use the IB curriculum, teaching more than one million students around the globe in more than 4,000 schools. The highly acclaimed program encourages students to think critically, challenge assumptions, develop research skills, and consider both their local and international environment.
